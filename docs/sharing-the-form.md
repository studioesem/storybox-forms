# STORYBOX Contributor Submission Form

A web form for collecting contributions (photos, archival material) and a copyright/consent declaration in one go. Replaces the old JotForm.

## The link to share

For the **Inner West Museum of Rugby League** project:

```
https://forms.storybox.co/iwc-rugby-league
```

Anyone with the link can submit — no login required. The form works on phone, tablet, and desktop.

## What it asks contributors for

- **Consent block** — their name and at least one of three copyright tickboxes (sole owner / permissions obtained / unsure of provenance)
- **Submission details** — title of the contribution, year of creation, who to credit
- **Their details** — full name, email (required), and optional phone + address
- **Signature** — drawn on screen with finger or mouse
- **Photos** — up to 5 images, JPG/PNG/WebP, 15 MB each
- **Under‑18 block** (only shown if they tick the box) — adds a parent/guardian signature
- **Authorisation paragraph and Privacy Policy link** before submit

The form turns the **Submit Contribution** button green-yellow once everything required is filled. Until then it stays grey and unresponsive.

## What contributors see when they submit

A simple "Thank you — your contribution has been received" screen with a STORYBOX.CO link.

## What you can tell contributors

- Their photos go to a private cloud folder, not a public album.
- Their submission won't appear publicly until a moderator reviews and approves it.
- We may follow up by email or phone if we need anything more.
- The form is mobile-friendly — they can do it standing in the museum or at home later.

## Common questions

**"How many photos can I upload?"** Up to 5, max 15 MB each.

**"What if I don't know who owns the copyright?"** Tick option C — "I am unsure of the provenance" — and we'll handle it in moderation.

**"My contribution is of a child."** Tick the under-18 box — a second signature box appears for the parent or guardian.

**"Can I submit videos?"** Not through this form yet. Videos go through a separate process — let the project team know.
